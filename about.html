UX Researcher · Product Thinker · ResearchOps Builder

I care about making difficult moments easier to navigate.

A lot of the problems I’m drawn to are the ones that weigh heavily on people’s lives. In healthcare especially, that means looking closely at the moments where stress is high, clarity is low, and the experience has to do more than simply function, it has to help.

I’m motivated by meaningful problems. I want my work to be known for helping solve something real, not just making something look better on the surface.

Perspective

My perspective comes from working across the whole product life cycle.

I’ve served in roles that span research, discovery, product design, delivery, oversight, and governance. That breadth matters because it helps me see how decisions travel, from the earliest questions to handoffs, execution, and what ultimately reaches the customer.

It also changes the kinds of questions I ask. I’m not interested in collecting interesting observations that go nowhere. I want to validate the signal, make it clear, and work with the team to decide what happens next.

Working style

How I show up on teams

I do my best work with teams that care deeply about solving real problems, teams that are willing to test assumptions, peel back the layers, and put in the effort to understand what will actually help the people they’re building for.

I prefer being embedded with a team rather than operating at a distance. The best work happens when research, design, product, and engineering are moving together.

Working with me

You can count on me to ask a lot of questions, the kind that cut through uncertainty and make the next decision clearer.

Beyond work

Meaningful work matters to me on and off the job.

After the Pulse nightclub tragedy in Orlando, my team and I organized a volunteer-led T-shirt fundraiser to support victims’ families. The effort raised $80,000. It’s not a conventional portfolio case study, but it reflects the kind of leadership I value most: moving quickly, creating something purposeful, and rallying people around a real need.
